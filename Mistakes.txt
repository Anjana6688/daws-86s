Steps for permission :
To assign the AdministratorAccess policy to an IAM user in AWS, follow these steps:
Sign in to the AWS Management Console: Use your root account or an IAM user with sufficient permissions to manage IAM users and policies.
Navigate to the IAM console: From the AWS Management Console, search for "IAM" in the search bar and select the IAM service.
Select Users: In the navigation pane on the left, under "Access management," choose "Users."
Choose the target IAM user: Click on the name of the IAM user to whom you want to assign AdministratorAccess.
Add Permissions: On the user's details page, select the "Permissions" tab.
Attach Policies Directly: Choose "Add permissions" and then select "Attach policies directly."
Search for AdministratorAccess: In the search bar provided, type "AdministratorAccess" and press Enter.
Select the policy: Check the box next to the AdministratorAccess AWS managed policy.
Add Permissions: Click "Add permissions" to attach the policy to the selected IAM user.
